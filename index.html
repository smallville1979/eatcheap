Ready Meals — The Biggest Rip Off

A ready meal costs £3-5. Has one portion. Low quality ingredients. High salt. You are paying for the packaging and the convenience — not the food.

For £3 you can buy: pasta, tinned tomatoes, cheese. That is 3 meals not one.

Ready meals = paying £3 for £1 worth of food in a plastic tray. Every single time.

Branded Products

Heinz beans, Kellogg's cornflakes, Hovis bread. You are paying for the logo. The own brand version in most supermarkets is made in the same factory.

Own brand everything. Always. The logo adds nothing to your stomach.

Meal Deals and Lunch Runs

£4-6 every weekday for lunch = £100 a month. Make your own. Bring it. Two slices of bread, cheese, done. 20p versus £5. Every day.

YELLOW STICKER TIMING

Supermarkets reduce food to almost nothing before closing. If you time it right, you can get £10-15 of food for under £2. This is not luck — it is a schedule you can plan around.

Turn up 30–45 minutes before closing. Ask staff directly. They know exactly when reductions happen.

~7PM–9PM

MORRISONS

~6PM–8PM

ASDA

~7PM–9PM

THE BILLS
RULE

this one change stops the end of month panic permanently.

Direct Debit. Same Day. Every Time.

Set every single bill as a direct debit. Set the payment date to your payday — or the day after. Rent, gas, electric, phone, internet — all of it.

When it comes out the same day you get paid, you never see it. You never spend it. What is left in your account after that is your actual money.

Pay yourself last. Bills come first. What is left is yours to live on. No surprises. No panic.

The Maths Changes Everything

Most people get paid, feel rich, spend freely for 2 weeks, then panic for 2 weeks. The direct debit trick flips it — you know exactly what you have from day one.

If your bills total £600 and you earn £1200 — you have £600. Know that on day one. Not day fourteen.

Build a Small Buffer Too

Once bills are sorted — put even £10-20 in a separate pot the same day. Do not touch it. This is your food emergency fund. If you run low mid-month, it is there.

Bills pot. Buffer pot. The rest is yours. Simple system. Works every time.

📋 MEAL PLANNING — DO THIS ON SUNDAY BEFORE YOU SHOP

Five minutes of planning before you go out saves you £20+ every week. Know what you are buying and what meals you are making from it. No random crap in the basket, no wasted food in the bin.

  1. Write down 5–7 meals for the week.
  2. List only the ingredients you need.
  3. Check what you already have in. Cross those off.
  4. Go to ASDA. Stick to the list. Walk past everything else.
  5. Grab yellow stickers and TG2G bags as a bonus — use them to reduce the list further.
